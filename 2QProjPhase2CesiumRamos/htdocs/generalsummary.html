The History of Germany


Summary of German History

After the collapse of the Western Roman Empire, the Germanic tribes of Europe became more powerful. They created states in what was once Rome. After some time, these states became Francia. This was succeeded by the Holy Roman Empire, a state composed of German States of varying size and influence. Among these states, Prussia and Austria emerged as the most powerful. After several wars under the guidance of Otto von Bismarck, Prussia unified with several other small states to form the North German Confederation. They went to war with France in 1870, ultimately winning. They united with all the other states (except Austria) and annexed the region of Alsace-Lorraine. This resulted in the renaming of the Confederation to the German Empire on the 10th of December 1871. Wilhelm I was proclaimed Emperor on the 18th of January 1871 in the Palace of Versailles. After Wilhelm I passed away, he was replaced by Friedrich III, who died 99 days after his accession to the throne. He was replaced by Wilhelm II. In 1914, Germany joined the side of Austria-Hungary against Serbia, France and others. They surrendered on the 11th of November 1918 at 11:00 AM. In 1919, the monarchy was replaced by a republic, known to modern historians as the Weimar Republic. During the Great Depression, the Republic suffered heavily. A charismatic politician by the name of Adolf Hitler and his party, the NSDAP, gained popularity. In 1933, the Reichstag burned. Hitler used this opportunity to take power and rule with his party until 1945. In 1939, Germany started World War II by invading Poland. The war in Europe ended in May of 1945. The eastern half of Germany was given to Poland while the Western half was split into 4 zones, those being the British, American, French and Soviet zones. The British, American and French zones became East Germany while the Soviet zone stayed in the Eastern Bloc as East Germany. In 1990, East Germany, like other Eastern Bloc countries, left the influence of the USSR and reunited with West Germany, forming the Federal Republic of Germany that we know today.
