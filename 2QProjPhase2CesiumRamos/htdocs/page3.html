The History of Germany


Post-WWII Germany

After World War II, Germany was divided. The eastern half was given to Poland while the western half was divided into 4 zones. The American, French and British zones were in the westernmost regions while the Soviet zone was in the eastern area of the half. Berlin, despite being in the middle of East Germany, was split into East and West regions. The American, French and British zones were combined in 1949 to form West Germany. West Germany was aligned with the USA and the west while East Germany was aligned with the Soviet Union. In 1990, amidst the collapse of several communist states in Europe, people crossed from East Berlin into West Berlin. This led to the reunification of the country leading to the Germany of the modern age.
