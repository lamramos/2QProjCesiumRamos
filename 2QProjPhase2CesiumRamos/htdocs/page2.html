The History of Germany


Pre- and WW2 Germany

On November 9, 1918, Kaiser Wilhelm II, the Kaiser of the German Empire, abdicated. This marked the end of the Hohenzollern monarchy and the German Empire. This led to a period of turmoil between 1918-1919, fought by the government, referred to by modern historians as the Weimar Republic, and various far-left radicals such as the Spartacus League. The government ultimately won and the constitution of the new republic was implemented on August 11, 1919. The new government changed the administrative divisions of the country. The nation was now a democratic republic. The president of the nation was a man who had many powers. After World War I, Germany lost a significant amount of territory. A certain party called the NSDAP appealed to the public by blaming their strife on scapegoats such as Jews and promising to rebuild the nation. The head of said party was a man named Adolf Hitler. Amidst panic in 1933, the Enabling Act was passed. This allowed Adolf Hitler and his NSDAP to take control of the nation. This was the end of the Weimar era. The regime of Adolf Hitler was characterized by antisemitism, racism and overall brutality. In 1939 Germany invaded Poland, starting the Second World War. Germany ultimately lost this war in 1945. The war in Europe ended on May 8, 1945.
