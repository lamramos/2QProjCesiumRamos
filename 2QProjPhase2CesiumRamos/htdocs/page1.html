The History of Germany


The German Empire (1871-1918)

The German Empire, also called the Second Reich or Kaiserreich, was an empire that existed from 1871 to 1918. It was formed in the aftermath of the Franco-Prussian War, in which Prussia, the leading German power in Europe, was victorious. One of the main players in the formation of the German Empire was Otto von Bismarck, a diplomat who pushed Prussia towards unification of the German states. The events that would lead to unification began with the Second Schleswig War. Here, Prussia and Austria defeated Denmark and acquired territory in Schleswig, the southern part of Jutland. They had some disputes over control of Schleswig which led to the Austro-Prussian War. Prussia won once again, annexing smaller German states aligned with Austria and forming the North German Confederation. In 1870, due to rising tensions with France, France declared war on the North German Confederation. In 1914, Gavrilo Princip, a Serb, shot Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian monarchy, and his wife. This lead Austria-Hungary to declare war on Serbia. Due to a large web of alliances, the nations of Europe entered war, what we now call World War I. Germany joined the side of Austria-Hungary alongside the Ottoman Empire, Bulgaria and others. On the 11th of November 1918, an armistice was signed. This marked the end of WWI.
